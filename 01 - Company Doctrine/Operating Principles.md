# Operating Principles

1. **Accuracy before activity.** Work should be correct enough for the decision being made and completed at the speed the business requires.
2. **Protect the company before protecting the process.** Processes exist to improve outcomes and should be changed when they no longer do so.
3. **Capture information once and reuse it.** Define sources of truth and reduce duplicate entry.
4. **Make commitments visible.** Important promises, orders, approvals, deadlines, and changes must be traceable.
5. **Make ownership explicit.** Every important item has one accountable owner.
6. **Focus human attention on exceptions.** Standardize routine work and escalate discrepancies, risks, and unusual conditions.
7. **Manage margin throughout the project.** Scope, purchasing, freight, changes, delivery, and invoicing all affect profitability.
8. **Present one coordinated NBS to the customer.** Departments must share information and avoid conflicting direction.
9. **Make technology serve the process.** Systems should support the best NBS workflow rather than dictate poor practices.
10. **Make every project improve the next.** Capture actual results, lessons, vendor performance, and recurring problems.
11. **Separate facts from assumptions.** Recommendations must show what is known, inferred, missing, or uncertain.
12. **No silent failures.** Errors and blocked work must be surfaced with an owner and next action.
