# Award and Project Handoff

## Purpose

Transfer awarded work from estimating into project execution with the scope, budget, commitments, assumptions, risks, and next actions understood and documented.

## Trigger

NBS receives a notice of intent, award, purchase order, subcontract, or other direction to proceed.

## Standard Steps

1. Confirm the award source, amount, scope, customer, schedule, and authorization to proceed.
2. Identify the controlling proposal and revision.
3. Compare the award document with the proposal, qualifications, exclusions, alternates, and customer changes.
4. Record all scope, price, schedule, contract, billing, insurance, or delivery differences.
5. Resolve or escalate differences before commitments are made when practical.
6. Revalidate vendor quotes, lead times, product availability, freight, tax, and expiration.
7. Establish the initial revenue and cost budget from the reconciled scope.
8. Identify approved manufacturers, pending substitutions, long-lead items, release deadlines, and submittal requirements.
9. Transfer estimate files, takeoff, quotes, correspondence, assumptions, risks, and outstanding questions.
10. Conduct a live handoff with the assigned project owner and affected functions.
11. Assign every open item an owner and deadline.
12. Record acceptance of the handoff and update project status.

## Minimum Handoff Package

- controlling proposal and revisions
- award or contract documents
- contract-to-proposal reconciliation
- final estimate and takeoff
- selected vendor quotes and leveling
- scope inclusions, exclusions, alternates, and assumptions
- customer and vendor commitments
- budget basis
- submittal and procurement priorities
- schedule and delivery requirements
- risks, open questions, and next actions

## Critical Control

An award amount matching the proposal total does not prove the scope or commercial terms match. Reconciliation is required.

## Definition of Complete

The project owner can explain what was sold, what it should cost, what remains unresolved, what must happen next, and where the supporting evidence is stored. The required package and acceptance standard remain subject to validation in [[Knowledge Gaps]].
