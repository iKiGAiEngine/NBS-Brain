# Plan and Specification Review

## Purpose

Determine the complete intended scope, approved products, locations, quantities to be measured, and conflicts requiring qualification or clarification.

## Review Sequence

1. Confirm the document set, issue dates, and addenda received.
2. Review Division 00 and 01 requirements that affect specialties, pricing, schedule, alternates, substitutions, closeout, freight, or taxes.
3. Review applicable specifications and approved manufacturers.
4. Review floor plans, reflected ceiling plans, elevations, enlarged plans, schedules, details, and legends.
5. Cross-reference plan callouts with specifications and product schedules.
6. Identify delegated design, coordination, blocking, installation, field measurement, and responsibility boundaries.
7. Record conflicts, omissions, ambiguous scope, and potential substitutions.
8. Build the takeoff checklist by category, callout, location, and document reference.
9. Determine which questions require an RFI, vendor input, assumption, exclusion, or qualification.

## Evidence Standard

Every material scope conclusion should cite its plan sheet, detail, schedule, specification section, addendum, or written direction.

## Exceptions and Escalation

- plans and specifications conflict
- a named product is unavailable, obsolete, or outside territory
- responsibility between trades is unclear
- installation or field conditions materially affect material scope
- addenda appear incomplete or contradictory

## Definition of Complete

Applicable scope is mapped to document evidence; approved manufacturers, alternates, exclusions, risks, and questions are recorded; and the quantity takeoff has a complete checklist.
