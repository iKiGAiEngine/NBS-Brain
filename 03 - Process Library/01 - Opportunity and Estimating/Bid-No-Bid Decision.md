# Bid-No-Bid Decision

## Purpose

Use limited estimating capacity on opportunities that fit NBS capabilities, relationships, strategy, schedule, risk tolerance, and profit potential.

## Decision Inputs

- customer and relationship value
- project location and delivery requirements
- requested product categories and scope fit
- document quality and available estimating time
- bid date and project schedule
- manufacturer and vendor coverage
- likely competition and award path
- contract, payment, logistics, or unusual risk
- strategic value and probability of award

## Standard Steps

1. Confirm minimum project information is available.
2. Assess scope fit and identify specialist support required.
3. Evaluate workload and time available for a quality estimate.
4. Identify major commercial, schedule, location, and vendor risks.
5. Estimate strategic value and reasonable award probability.
6. Decide: bid, bid with conditions, decline, or request more information.
7. Record the decision, reasoning, owner, and next action.
8. Communicate the response when needed.

## Proposed Decision Principle

Pursue work when NBS can create a defensible scope and price, reasonably execute the project, protect margin, and create strategic or financial value. Do not bid merely because an invitation exists.

## Escalate When

- scope or contract risk is outside normal work
- required estimating effort is unusually high
- schedule makes a responsible proposal unlikely
- the opportunity requires a leadership-level relationship decision
- authority for the decision is unclear

## Definition of Complete

A documented decision exists with rationale, decision owner, conditions, estimator assignment or decline action, and the next deadline.
