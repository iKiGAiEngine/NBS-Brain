# Estimate Setup

## Purpose

Create a controlled estimate workspace with the correct documents, scope structure, responsibilities, deadlines, and review plan.

## Required Inputs

- approved bid decision and conditions
- assigned estimator
- current plans, specifications, addenda, and bidder instructions
- bid deadline and internal review deadline
- requested scope and alternates
- customer and delivery information

## Standard Steps

1. Create the estimate record using the approved naming and numbering standard.
2. Link it to the single opportunity record.
3. Store or link the current bid documents without replacing prior versions.
4. Create the scope and product-category checklist.
5. Record alternates, unit prices, substitutions, and special forms required.
6. Establish internal dates for takeoff, RFQs, follow-up, review, and submission.
7. Identify support needed from other estimators, vendors, project management, logistics, or leadership.
8. Create a risk and missing-information list.
9. Confirm the estimator can access all required systems and documents.

## Control Rules

- One authoritative estimate record per proposal revision.
- Document versions and addenda must remain traceable.
- Unknowns must be recorded rather than silently assumed.
- Internal review must occur before the external deadline.

## Definition of Complete

The estimate has an assigned owner, linked opportunity, controlled documents, scope structure, milestone dates, known requirements, and an active missing-information and risk list.
