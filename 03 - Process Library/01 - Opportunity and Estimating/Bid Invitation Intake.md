# Bid Invitation Intake

## Purpose

Capture each legitimate opportunity once, establish the current requirements and deadline, and assign ownership for qualification.

## Trigger

A bid invitation, customer request, plan-room notice, referral, or direct project inquiry is received.

## Required Inputs

- project name and location
- customer and contact
- bid due date, time, and time zone
- requested scope
- document-access location
- delivery or regional requirements
- invitation source and date received

## Standard Steps

1. Confirm the invitation is legitimate and within NBS scope.
2. Search for an existing opportunity using project name, location, customer, and bid date.
3. Create or update one opportunity record; do not create a duplicate.
4. Record the invitation details and source.
5. Obtain access to plans, specifications, addenda, and bidder instructions.
6. Identify missing information or access problems.
7. Assign a qualification owner.
8. Set the next action and decision deadline before the bid due date.
9. Acknowledge receipt when required.

## Exceptions and Escalation

- Possible duplicate with conflicting information
- Bid date is unclear or has already passed
- Documents cannot be accessed
- Requested scope is outside normal NBS coverage
- Customer, location, schedule, or workload creates unusual risk

## Definition of Complete

The opportunity has one record, a qualification owner, known bid deadline, accessible document source or tracked access issue, requested scope, and a scheduled bid/no-bid decision.

## Automation Opportunity

Extract invitation fields, check duplicates, create the record, assign default status, and alert on missing or conflicting information. See [[Automation Opportunity Register]].
