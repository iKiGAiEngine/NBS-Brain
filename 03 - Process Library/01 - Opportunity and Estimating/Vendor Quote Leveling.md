# Vendor Quote Leveling

## Purpose

Compare vendor offers on an equivalent scope and identify the true commercial, schedule, and execution differences before selecting estimate costs.

## Standard Steps

1. Confirm each quote references the correct project, document revision, and RFQ scope.
2. Compare quoted quantities with the NBS takeoff.
3. Normalize units, pack sizes, freight, tax treatment, and alternates.
4. Map every inclusion, exclusion, qualification, substitution, and allowance.
5. Compare manufacturer, model, finish, accessories, warranty, and compliance.
6. Compare quote validity, escalation terms, lead time, production assumptions, and delivery terms.
7. Identify missing items, duplicated scope, arithmetic errors, and suspicious pricing variances.
8. Obtain clarifications in writing and retain quote revisions.
9. Select or recommend the pricing basis using total risk-adjusted value, not price alone.
10. Record the chosen cost, rejected alternatives, assumptions, and unresolved risks.

## Escalate When

- the lowest quote is materially incomplete or noncompliant
- quote quantities differ significantly from NBS takeoff
- product substitution affects approval or performance
- lead time cannot support the project
- pricing variance lacks a reasonable explanation

## Definition of Complete

Selected estimate costs are traceable to reviewed quotes or approved budget sources, and all material differences, assumptions, and risks are documented.
