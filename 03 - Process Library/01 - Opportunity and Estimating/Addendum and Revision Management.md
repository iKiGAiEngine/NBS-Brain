# Addendum and Revision Management

## Purpose

Ensure every document or customer change is evaluated, incorporated, priced, communicated, and traceable without losing the prior proposal basis.

## Trigger

- addendum, bulletin, revised drawing, or specification is issued
- customer requests a revision, alternate, clarification, or value-engineering option
- vendor revises pricing or availability
- NBS discovers an error or material assumption change

## Standard Steps

1. Record the change source, date, revision identifier, and response deadline.
2. Preserve the prior document, estimate, quote, and proposal versions.
3. Compare changed documents and identify affected scope, quantities, products, schedule, and commercial terms.
4. Notify affected vendors and obtain revised pricing or written confirmation.
5. Update the takeoff and estimate using traceable change notes.
6. Re-evaluate risk, lead time, validity, freight, tax, and margin.
7. Prepare a clearly numbered proposal revision or addendum acknowledgment.
8. Obtain the required review and approval.
9. Submit and retain evidence.
10. Update the opportunity record and communicate superseded versions.

## Revision Control Rules

- Never overwrite the prior submitted proposal.
- Every revision must explain what changed and what remains unchanged.
- Verbal direction affecting scope, price, or schedule must be documented.
- Late changes that prevent responsible pricing must be qualified or escalated.

## Definition of Complete

The latest proposal basis is identifiable, all material changes are documented and priced or qualified, prior versions remain available, and affected parties know which version controls.
