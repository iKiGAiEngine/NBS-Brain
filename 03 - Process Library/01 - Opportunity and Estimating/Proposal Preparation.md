# Proposal Preparation

## Purpose

Convert the reviewed estimate into a clear commercial offer that accurately communicates the scope, price, assumptions, and boundaries of NBS responsibility.

## Standard Steps

1. Confirm the customer, project, bid date, proposal revision, and current documents.
2. Reconcile estimate quantities and selected vendor costs.
3. Apply approved cost treatment for material, freight, tax, escalation, contingency, and other project-specific costs.
4. Apply approved markup or margin logic and document exceptions.
5. Organize the proposal by scope or product category in a way the customer can evaluate.
6. State included manufacturers, models, quantities or allowances as appropriate.
7. Separate base bid, alternates, unit prices, options, and voluntary substitutions.
8. State exclusions, qualifications, delivery assumptions, quote validity, and schedule limitations.
9. Confirm addenda acknowledged and customer forms completed.
10. Cross-check the proposal total with the estimate and approval summary.
11. Save a controlled draft for review.

## Proposal Quality Standard

A reader should understand what NBS is providing, what it is not providing, which assumptions affect price, and how to request clarification without reconstructing the estimate.

## Common Risk Checks

- installation accidentally included or implied
- accessories, freight, tax, or trim omitted
- alternates mixed into base scope
- vendor exclusions not carried into the proposal
- outdated quote or document revision used
- proposal wording conflicts with the estimate
- price validity does not match vendor exposure

## Definition of Complete

A complete draft proposal is reconciled to the estimate, contains required commercial terms and scope boundaries, and is ready for independent review.
