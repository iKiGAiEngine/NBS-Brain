# Quantity Takeoff

## Purpose

Produce traceable quantities that align with the identified scope, product requirements, locations, units, and proposal structure.

## Standard Steps

1. Confirm the current document revision and takeoff checklist.
2. Define the counting or measurement method for each category.
3. Record plan callout, description, model or basis of design, quantity, unit, location, and drawing reference.
4. Separate base bid, alternates, allowances, options, and excluded scope.
5. Include required accessories, trim, hardware, supports, spares, and related components when supported by documents or approved rules.
6. Track assumptions for unclear or incomplete conditions.
7. Reconcile counts across plans, elevations, schedules, details, and specifications.
8. Review unusual quantities, duplicates, zero-count callouts, and unit conversions.
9. Save the takeoff version and reviewer evidence.

## Quality Checks

- Every relevant callout is accounted for.
- Units match vendor and proposal units.
- Linear, square, and each quantities are not mixed.
- Accessories and trim use documented calculation logic.
- Alternates are not included in base bid unless directed.
- Addendum changes can be identified by comparison.

## Exceptions and Escalation

- documents provide conflicting counts
- measurement depends on field verification
- product configuration cannot be determined
- count differs materially from schedules or vendor interpretation

## Definition of Complete

Quantities are traceable, categorized, reviewed for completeness and unit consistency, and ready to support RFQs and estimate pricing.
