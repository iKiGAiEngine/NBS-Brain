# Opportunity and Estimating Overview

## Purpose

Convert a qualified bid opportunity into a complete, defensible, competitive proposal and a controlled handoff if awarded.

## Workflow

1. [[Bid Invitation Intake]]
2. [[Bid-No-Bid Decision]]
3. [[Estimate Setup]]
4. [[Plan and Specification Review]]
5. [[Quantity Takeoff]]
6. [[Vendor RFQ and Follow-Up]]
7. [[Vendor Quote Leveling]]
8. [[Proposal Preparation]]
9. [[Proposal Review and Approval]]
10. [[Addendum and Revision Management]]
11. [[Award and Project Handoff]]

## Required Outcomes

- The opportunity is recorded once and assigned.
- The current bid documents and deadlines are known.
- Scope, quantities, vendor coverage, and assumptions are traceable.
- Quotes are compared on an equivalent basis.
- The proposal clearly states inclusions, exclusions, alternates, qualifications, and validity.
- Required review occurs before submission.
- Revisions and addenda are controlled.
- Awarded scope and budget are handed off without relying on estimator memory.

## Control Points

- bid/no-bid approval
- document and addendum version control
- takeoff completeness review
- vendor coverage and quote expiration
- proposal risk and authority review
- submission confirmation
- contract-to-proposal reconciliation at award

## Primary Records

- opportunity record
- bid-document register
- scope checklist
- takeoff and estimate
- RFQ and vendor quote log
- quote-leveling record
- proposal and revisions
- submission evidence
- award reconciliation and handoff package
