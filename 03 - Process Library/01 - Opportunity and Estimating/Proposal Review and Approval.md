# Proposal Review and Approval

## Purpose

Identify scope, cost, commercial, and presentation errors before NBS makes an external commitment.

## Review Inputs

- current proposal draft
- estimate summary and takeoff
- quote-leveling record and selected vendor quotes
- addendum register
- risk and assumption list
- required customer forms

## Standard Review

1. Confirm project, customer, revision, deadline, and acknowledged documents.
2. Verify proposal scope matches the estimate and takeoff.
3. Check major quantities, extensions, totals, freight, tax, escalation, and margin logic.
4. Confirm vendor exclusions and unresolved risks are addressed.
5. Review alternates, substitutions, unit prices, and validity.
6. Verify delivery, installation, field measurement, schedule, and responsibility language.
7. Confirm approvals required by value, margin, risk, customer, or exception.
8. Correct findings and record material changes.
9. Obtain approval and lock the submission version.
10. Submit through the required channel and retain confirmation.

## Review Levels

The required level should be determined by approved business rules based on proposal value, margin, unusual scope, contractual risk, strategic importance, and estimator experience. Thresholds remain an open item in [[Knowledge Gaps]].

## No-Silent-Approval Rule

Absence of feedback is not approval. The review record should identify who reviewed, what was checked, findings, corrections, and final approval.

## Definition of Complete

The approved version was submitted on time, submission evidence is stored, the opportunity status is updated, and follow-up ownership is assigned.
