# Vendor RFQ and Follow-Up

## Purpose

Obtain timely, complete, comparable, and traceable vendor pricing for the required scope.

## RFQ Package

- project and bid deadline
- required vendor return date and time zone
- project location and delivery assumptions
- applicable plans, specifications, schedules, and addenda
- structured quantity and scope list
- requested alternates or substitutions
- required freight, tax, lead-time, validity, and exclusion information
- NBS contact and response method

## Standard Steps

1. Select vendors based on product authorization, territory, capability, performance, and approved sourcing strategy.
2. Issue a consistent RFQ package with version identification.
3. Record vendor, contact, issue date, scope, document revision, and required return date.
4. Confirm receipt for critical or high-risk scope.
5. Track intent to quote, declines, questions, and missing coverage.
6. Send follow-ups based on the planned cadence rather than estimator memory.
7. Route vendor questions and clarifications to all affected pricing sources when needed.
8. Store each quote and revision without overwriting the prior version.
9. Escalate uncovered scope before the proposal review deadline.

## Quote Completeness Request

Require the vendor to identify inclusions, exclusions, quantities, unit prices when available, freight, tax treatment, lead time, quote validity, alternates, substitutions, and assumptions.

## Definition of Complete

Every required scope has a quote, defensible budget source, documented exclusion, or escalated coverage gap; quote versions and vendor communications are traceable.

## Automation Opportunity

Generate RFQs from structured takeoff data, track responses, schedule follow-ups, detect missing scope, and summarize vendor exceptions.
