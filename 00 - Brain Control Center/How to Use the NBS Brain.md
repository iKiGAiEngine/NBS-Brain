# How to Use the NBS Brain

## Purpose

Use the Brain to find approved company direction, understand how work should move, capture decisions, and improve future performance.

## Navigation

Begin at [[NBS Brain Home]]. Follow links rather than relying only on folders. Each note should connect to its governing principle, related process, responsible role, and supporting decisions.

## Add Information in the Correct Form

- A permanent company belief belongs in Company Doctrine.
- A repeatable workflow belongs in the Process Library.
- A mandatory requirement or tolerance belongs in Business Rules.
- A one-time choice belongs in Decisions.
- A reusable result from experience belongs in Lessons Learned.
- A missing answer belongs in [[Knowledge Gaps]].
- A software behavior belongs in AiPM System Design.

## Evidence Labels

When adding important information, identify it as one of the following:

- **Confirmed fact** — supported by an authoritative source.
- **Approved rule** — formally accepted by the correct decision-maker.
- **Current practice** — describes what happens today but may not be the desired future state.
- **Assumption** — believed to be true but not confirmed.
- **Recommendation** — proposed direction awaiting approval.
- **Open question** — information still needed.

## Safe Use

Do not place confidential project data, customer pricing, contracts, employee information, credentials, or vendor discount information in a public repository. Use placeholders until the repository is private and company handling is approved.

## Updating a Process

1. Identify the current process note.
2. Record the problem or improvement opportunity.
3. Confirm the process owner and affected roles.
4. Update the proposed future state.
5. Record the decision in a Decision Record.
6. Update related business rules, templates, training, and AiPM requirements.
7. Set a review date and measure the result.

## Definition of Complete

A Brain note is complete when it has an owner, status, evidence level, related links, approval state, and review cycle appropriate to its importance.
