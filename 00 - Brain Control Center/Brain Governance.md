# Brain Governance

## Purpose

Governance keeps the NBS Brain reliable, current, secure, and useful. The Brain should never become an uncontrolled collection of conflicting notes.

## Authority Levels

1. **Approved** — accepted by the responsible leader or process owner.
2. **Proposed** — recommended but not yet approved.
3. **Current practice** — documents the present workflow without declaring it best practice.
4. **Historical** — retained for context but no longer active.
5. **Retired** — intentionally replaced and not used for current decisions.

## Required Note Properties

Important notes should include:

- type
- status
- owner
- review cycle
- classification
- approval state
- effective date when applicable

## Source-of-Truth Rules

Each major data category must have one defined authoritative source. When information conflicts, do not silently choose one version. Record the conflict, identify the controlling source, and request a decision when necessary.

General authority order:

1. Executed contract or approved change
2. Approved submittal or written customer direction
3. Current construction documents and addenda
4. Approved NBS business rule or leadership decision
5. NBS proposal and qualifications
6. Accepted vendor quote or purchase order
7. Internal note, memory, or assumption

The correct hierarchy may vary by issue and does not replace contractual review.

## Change Control

Material changes must record:

- what changed
- why it changed
- who approved it
- effective date
- affected processes and roles
- required training or system updates
- expected measure of success

## Security

Information classification:

- Public
- Internal framework
- Confidential business
- Restricted financial or personnel

A repository being technically private does not automatically mean all company data is approved for storage there.

## Review Cadence

- Monthly: operational dashboards and active gaps
- Quarterly: processes, rules, and performance measures
- Annually: company doctrine, strategy, authority, and structure
- Event-driven: after major errors, organizational changes, system changes, or lessons learned

## Human Approval Boundary

AI may summarize, compare, identify risks, draft recommendations, and propose changes. Authorized people must approve contractual, financial, legal, personnel, customer-facing, and policy commitments.
